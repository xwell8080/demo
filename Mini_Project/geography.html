Geographic Profile of Batac City

Batac City, located in the province of Ilocos Norte, Philippines, is situated in the northern part of the island of Luzon. The city is known for its flat terrain and its proximity to the majestic Mount Sicapoo. Batac is bordered by neighboring municipalities such as Laoag City to the west and Paoay to the south. The city enjoys a tropical climate with distinct wet and dry seasons.

Batac's strategic location along the national highway makes it a key point for travelers heading to other parts of Ilocos Norte, including the famous Paoay Church and the white sand beaches of Pagudpud. The city's agricultural landscape is rich, with rice fields and vegetable farms dotting the area. Batac is also known for its traditional farming practices, which contribute to its local economy.
